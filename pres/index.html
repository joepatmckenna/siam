Loss of Ca$^{2+}$ oscillations

Nikita Mukhitov, Florida State Univeristy Chemistry

  • Calcium oscillations are terminated by raising glucose above an upper threshold
  • Upper threshold varies from islet to islet
  • About 2/3 of islets tested exhibit slow oscillations, period > 2min
  • We will see that delivering oscillatory glucose can recover these lost calcium oscillations
  • Furthermore, we will see that other oscillating islets acting through the liver can recover these lost calcium oscillations

Modeling

$\beta$-cell Dual Oscillator Model (Bertram et al, Biophys J 2004)

  • Glycolytic oscillations drive bursting and Ca$^{2+}$ oscillations
  • Glycolytic oscillator incorporated because of data from muscle extract, validated in beta-cells by data from PKAR sensor

Glycolytic oscillator

\[\boxed{\begin{aligned} {dG_i \over dt} & = J_{GLUT}(G_e)-J_{GK} \\ {dF6P \over dt} & = \alpha_1(J_{GK} - J_{PFK}) \\ {dFBP \over dt} & = \alpha_2(J_{PFK} - J_{PDH}) \end{aligned}}\]
  • Glucose taken up by glucose transporters
  • Glucose phosphorylated by GK
  • F6P converted to FBP by PFK
  • FBP allostreically activates PFK
  • External glucose is parameter that we will use to drive the system

Glycolytic oscillations

  • FBP activates PFK -> consumes F6P -> FBP consumed downstream -> PFK inactivates
  • For which values of external glucose and for which initial conditions do glycolytic oscillations occur?

Glycolytic bifurcations

  • At low glucose, no oscillations
  • Definitely oscillations at intermediate level
  • Oscillations may be lost in bistable range
  • Oscillations definitely lost at high levels
  • The Hopf at Gmid and the bistable range are critical for the following

Glycolytic bifurcations

  • For a small range, an unstable periodic orbit separates a stable quilibrium from a stable steady state

Activating glycolytic oscillations

  • Depends on amplitude and frequency
  • Amplitude must be large enough to exit bistable range
  • Period must be resonant with period of glycolytic oscillator

Amplitude dependence

  • Amplitude must be large enough to cross below the Hopf

Period dependence

  • Period must be large enough to allow phase point to escape stable equilibrium domain of attraction
  • i.e. period must close to natural period of glycolytic oscillator, approx 5 min

Recovering Ca$^{2+}$ oscillations

  • Activating GO underlies recover of bursting and caclcium oscillations and hence pulsatile insulin secretion
